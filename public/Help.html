Help Center

Everything you need to know about booking your seat with PolyCoach.

1. Create Your Account

Click Register on the Home Page and fill in your details — your full name, program (e.g. BEEE4), phone number, and destination. That is all you need.

Once submitted you will be taken to a confirmation page. You are then ready to book.

Already registered before? You can go straight to Book — no need to register again.

2. Book Your Seat

Click Book on the Home Page. Fill in your destination, full name, phone number, and program. You will then be asked whether you are booking for yourself or for someone else.

Booking for a friend or family member? Choose Book for Someone Else and enter their name. Your details are still used for the payment.

3. Pay the Booking Fee

Send K5,000 as a booking fee to secure your seat using any of the payment options shown on the payment page — National Bank, Airtel Money, or TNM Mpamba.

Take a screenshot of your mobile money confirmation and upload it on the next page. You cannot proceed without uploading your proof of payment.

This is a booking fee to secure your seat, not the full bus fare. Make sure your screenshot clearly shows the transaction.

4. Choose Your Seat

After uploading your payment, you will see the bus layout. Green seats are available — click any green seat to select it, then confirm your booking.

Once confirmed your seat will appear as pending while your payment is being reviewed.

5. Payment Verification

After you submit your booking, your proof of payment is reviewed. Once verified, your seat is confirmed and a receipt will be made available to you with your seat number and booking details.

Contact us on the details below if you have not received confirmation within a reasonable time.

6. View Bus Status

You can check which seats are available, pending, or booked at any time by clicking View Bus Status on the Home Page. No login required — the bus layout is open for everyone to see.

Hover over any seat to see the name of the person who has booked it.
